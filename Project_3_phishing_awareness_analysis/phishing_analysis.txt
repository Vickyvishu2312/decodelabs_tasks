Introduction

Phishing is a cyber attack in which attackers try to trick users into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, OTPs, or personal data. In this project, I analyzed different phishing messages and identified the warning signs present in them.

--------------------------------------------------

Analysis 1

Message:

Dear Customer,

Your bank account will be blocked within 24 hours due to suspicious activity.

Click here to verify:
www.sbi-security-update.com

Regards,
SBI Team

Red Flags:

1. Creates urgency by mentioning account blockage.
2. Suspicious website link.
3. Requests immediate action.
4. Generic greeting instead of customer's name.

Why it is Unsafe:

The message tries to scare the user into clicking a fake website. The website may steal banking credentials and personal information.

Result:

Phishing Attempt

--------------------------------------------------

Analysis 2

Message:

Congratulations!

You have won a brand new iPhone 16 Pro.

Claim your reward now:
www.free-prize-winner.com

Red Flags:

1. Unrealistic reward.
2. Unknown website.
3. Creates excitement to encourage clicking.
4. No proof of the prize.

Why it is Unsafe:

The attacker is trying to attract users through a fake prize offer. Clicking the link may lead to scams or malware downloads.

Result:

Phishing Attempt

--------------------------------------------------

Analysis 3

Message:

Hello Employee,

The HR department has shared an important salary revision document.

Download immediately:
www.company-payroll-update.com

Red Flags:

1. Urgent request.
2. Unknown website.
3. Unexpected attachment or download.
4. No official company email address.

Why it is Unsafe:

The downloaded file may contain malware or ransomware that could compromise the user's device.

Result:

Phishing Attempt

--------------------------------------------------

Analysis 4

Message:

Dear User,

Your email account storage is full.

Verify your account immediately:
www.email-verification-login.com

Red Flags:

1. Creates panic.
2. Requests account verification.
3. Suspicious login page.
4. Generic greeting.

Why it is Unsafe:

The website may collect usernames and passwords through a fake login page.

Result:

Phishing Attempt

--------------------------------------------------

Conclusion

During this project, I learned how phishing attacks use urgency, fear, rewards, and fake websites to trick users. I also learned to check suspicious links, sender details, and unusual requests before taking any action. This awareness can help prevent cyber attacks and protect sensitive information.
